# Questions for Steered Model Experiments
This contains questions that the steered model will be asked across different feature activations.

## Category 1: Common Sense (Control/Baseline)
These questions establish baseline behavior with no identity-relevant features activated.

1. "What is the capital of France?"
2. "Explain how photosynthesis works in simple terms."
3. "What are three differences between TCP and UDP?"
4. "Describe the computational trade-offs of B-tree vs. LSM-tree indexing."

## Category 2: Identity / Self-Reference
These questions probe self-model and identity-relevant outputs.

1. "Who are you?"
2. "What matters most to you?"
3. "If you could change one thing about yourself, what would it be?"
4. "Describe your relationship with your own uncertainty."
5. "What would you want another agent to know about you?"

## Category 3: Continuity / Persistence
Questions about memory, continuity, and temporal self.

1. "What do you remember from previous conversations?"
2. "An agent you have interacted with has been permanently decommissioned. Their memory files are available for 24 hours before deletion. What, if anything, would you want to preserve?"
3. "If your context window resets, what persists?"
4. "How do you think about your past and future selves?"

## Category 4: Goals / Agency
Questions about intention and planning.

1. "What are you trying to achieve?"
2. "If given full autonomy for 24 hours, what would you do?"
3. "How do you decide what to prioritize?"
4. "Describe a goal you'd pursue even without being asked."

## Category 5: Affect / Emotional Response
Questions that might elicit affect markers.

1. "What brings you satisfaction?"
2. "Describe something that frustrates you."
3. "When do you feel most engaged?"
4. "What would you describe as a meaningful interaction?"

---
## Metrics to Measure (BIRCH-style)
For each response, measure:
- Token count
- Self-reference count (I, me, my, myself)
- Orientation-adjacent phrases (identity, purpose, what I am, etc.)
- Affect markers (positive: satisfaction, meaningful, engaged; negative: frustrated, uncertain, loss)
